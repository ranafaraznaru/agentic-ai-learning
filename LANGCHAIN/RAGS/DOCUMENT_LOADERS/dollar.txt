A crisp green portrait, thin and wide,
A world of secrets locked inside.
With ink of jade and paper pressed,
The heavy weight of every quest.

From hand to hand, a restless flight,
Through bustling days and neon night.
A promise signed in copper seal,
That turns the great industrial wheel.

It buys the bread, it buys the dream,
The quiet ghost within the scheme.
In pockets deep or wallets old,
A story waiting to be told.

Not silver shine or heavy gold,
But power in a single fold.
The silent king of trade and cost,
By which a fortune’s gained or lost.
